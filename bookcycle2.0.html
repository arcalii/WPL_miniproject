The Midnight Chronicles

Chapter 1: The Disappearance

By Alex Rivers • Updated Apr 18, 2025

The rain fell in sheets, drumming against the windows of Detective Morgan Chase's office. It was past midnight, and the precinct was nearly empty. Only the distant hum of the cleaning crew's vacuum and the occasional ping of the elevator broke the silence.

Morgan stared at the case file spread across her desk, rubbing her temples. Five disappearances in three months. No bodies, no ransom demands, no pattern except for one detail – each victim had vanished on the night of a new moon.

"Still here, Chase?" Captain Reeves appeared in her doorway, coat draped over his arm. "It's almost one in the morning."

"Just going over the Moonlight case again," she replied, not looking up from the photographs of the missing persons. Two men, three women, ages ranging from 24 to 38. No obvious connections between them.

"You need rest. Fresh eyes in the morning."

Morgan nodded absently, but they both knew she wouldn't leave. Not when the new moon was coming in three days, and potentially another victim with it.

The captain lingered a moment longer before sighing. "Don't stay all night. That's an order."

After he left, Morgan pulled up the city map on her computer, marking each location where the victims were last seen. There had to be a pattern, some connection she was missing.

Her phone buzzed with a text message.

Unknown number: Looking in the wrong places, Detective.

Morgan froze, staring at the screen. She quickly typed back.

Morgan: Who is this?

Three dots appeared, then disappeared. Then another message:

Unknown number: The answers you seek are in the shadows where moonlight can't reach.

Morgan's heart raced as she tried to trace the number, but it came back as a burner phone. She grabbed her jacket and keys. Someone was watching her, knew about the case, possibly even the perpetrator themselves.

As she rushed to her car, the rain still pouring, she didn't notice the figure watching from across the street, standing perfectly still despite the downpour, their face obscured by the shadows.

The game had begun.
